# Account FAQ

## How do I create an account?

Creating an account on LLM Trust is quick and free. Click the "Sign Up" button in the top-right corner of any page, and you'll be presented with several registration options. You can sign up using your email address and a password, or use one of our supported OAuth providers including Google, GitHub, and Discord for a faster process. Choose the method that works best for you.

After registration, you'll receive a confirmation email if you signed up with an email address. Click the verification link in that email to activate your account. Once verified, you can immediately start saving models to your watchlist, leaving reviews, and personalizing your experience. The entire process takes less than two minutes, and you can update your profile details and preferences at any time from your account settings page.

## How do I reset my password?

If you've forgotten your password, go to the login page and click the "Forgot Password?" link. Enter the email address associated with your account, and we'll send you a password reset link within a few minutes. The link is valid for 24 hours for security reasons. Click it to set a new password — we recommend choosing a strong, unique password that you haven't used elsewhere.

If you don't receive the reset email, check your spam or junk folder. You can request a new reset link if the previous one has expired. For accounts created using OAuth providers (Google, GitHub, Discord), password management is handled through those services — resetting your LLM Trust password won't affect your third-party account credentials. If you continue to experience issues, contact our support team for assistance.

## What is the difference between Free and Pro?

The Free tier on LLM Trust provides full access to our model directory, search and filtering tools, model detail pages, community reviews, and educational content. Free users can save models to a watchlist, participate in discussions, and contribute reviews. It's a complete experience designed for individual developers, students, and anyone exploring the LLM ecosystem.

Pro subscriptions unlock advanced features aimed at teams and power users. These include API access for programmatic model queries, advanced benchmark analytics with historical trends, custom watchlist alerts when new models matching your criteria are published, priority model verification for your submissions, and early access to beta features. Pro also removes any rate limits on searches and API calls. For pricing details, visit our pricing page.

## How do I delete my account?

We're sorry to see you go, but we make account deletion straightforward. Navigate to your Account Settings page and scroll to the "Danger Zone" section at the bottom. Click "Delete Account" and confirm your decision by entering your password. This action is permanent and will remove all your data including saved models, reviews, watchlist entries, and API keys within 30 days in accordance with our data retention policy.

Before deleting, you may want to export your data — we provide a data export tool in Account Settings that packages your reviews, watchlists, and activity history into a downloadable file. If you're deleting your account due to a specific issue, we'd appreciate hearing from you through our feedback form first. Your input helps us improve the platform for everyone. Note that any public reviews you've written may be retained in anonymized form after account deletion.

## What are API keys for?

API keys allow Pro subscribers to access LLM Trust programmatically. With an API key, you can query our model database, retrieve detailed model specifications, pull benchmark data, and integrate LLM Trust information into your own applications, scripts, or CI/CD pipelines. This is particularly useful for teams building tooling around model selection or automating model evaluation workflows.

You can generate API keys from your Account Settings under the "API Access" section. Each key can be scoped with specific permissions and rate limits. We recommend creating separate keys for different applications and rotating them periodically for security. If a key is compromised, you can revoke it immediately from your dashboard. Our API documentation provides detailed endpoint descriptions, request formats, and code examples to help you get started quickly.
