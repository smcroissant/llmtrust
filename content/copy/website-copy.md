## Appendix: Copywriting Guidelines for LLM Trust

### Voice & Tone

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Speak like a smart colleague | Sound like a marketing department |
| Be specific (numbers, names, benchmarks) | Be vague ("best-in-class," "cutting-edge") |
| Acknowledge limitations honestly | Oversell or make promises we can't keep |
| Use developer vocabulary naturally | Force jargon to sound technical |
| Write short, punchy sentences | Write long, academic paragraphs |
| Lead with value to the user | Lead with features or company pride |

### Power Words for CTAs

- **Explore** (discovery-oriented, low commitment)
- **Download** (action-oriented, concrete)
- **Compare** (decision-support, practical)
- **Get Started** (low barrier, welcoming)
- **Upgrade** (implies progress, not spending)

### Words to Avoid

- ❌ "Cutting-edge" — overused, means nothing
- ❌ "Revolutionary" — unless we literally started a revolution
- ❌ "Best-in-class" — says who? By what measure?
- ❌ "Leverage" — corporate jargon
- ❌ "Synergy" — please no
- ❌ "World-class" — see "best-in-class"
- ✅ Instead: use specific, verifiable claims

### Formatting Rules

- Headlines: Title Case, max 60 characters for H1s
- Subtitles: Sentence case, max 160 characters
- Body text: Sentence case, paragraphs max 3-4 sentences
- CTAs: Verb-first, max 4 words, arrow for direction (→)
- Stats: Always formatted (200+, 500K+, 4.8/5)
